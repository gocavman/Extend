Getting Started

Start here.

▶

The Purpose of Extend

Extend your fitness tracking and planning. Log exercises, weights, sets, reps and durations. No subscriptions, no ads, no unnecessary bloat. Personal records, volume trends, streaks, muscle distribution, and more. Important stats and charts you want to focus on, are readily available where you want them.

What You Can Track

Olympic weights, kettlebells, dumbbells, cardio, bodyweight — any exercise you do. Build routines, log sessions, and watch your progress and personal records build over time. Track water intake. Add shoes and track their distance.

Customization

Make it yours. Rearrange the navigation bar(s), build a custom Dashboard with the stats and shortcuts you care about, choose light or dark theme. Customize what you see and how you access it.

Getting Started Fast

1. Review muscles. 2. Review equipment. 3. Review exercises (which can be linked to muscles and equipment). 4. Build workout routines. Standard exercise sets, complexes, supersets, circuits. Lots of options to choose from. Need a voice to speak while working out? Explore the Voice Trainer module. Just need a timer? Explore a variety in the Timer module. 5. Do a workout! Want one selected randomly for you, explore the Generate Workout module. Want to do a single exercise quickly? Tap ▶ on any exercise in the Exercises module. a. Start/Control from your watch (iPhone/iPad can be close by or not). b. Start/Control from your iPhone/iPad (will mirror on your watch). 6. Save what you've completed and review your progress in the Log and in all related modules. 7. Optionally, set up a Training Plan to schedule workouts and activities across your week.

Settings

Theme, units, navigation, dashboard, iCloud, HealthKit, Water, and data import/export.

▶

Navigation Bar(s)

Add, remove, and reorder modules across the top and bottom navigation bars. Each bar is configured independently. • Color — set a background color, optional gradient, gradient direction, and text color. • Customize — opens the full nav bar editor to add, remove, and reorder module icons.

Dashboard

Configure the appearance and contents of your Dashboard. • Color — set a background color and optional gradient for the Dashboard. • Customize — opens the tile editor to add, remove, resize, and reorder tiles. Tile types: • Module Shortcut — opens a module. • Stat Card — shows a live stat. • Shortcut — launches a specific workout, timer, voice trainer, or exercise. • Blank — fill in space with an animated emoticon. Stat cards available: Today's Plan, Favorite Exercise, Favorite Day, Workout Frequency, Muscle Group Distribution (7 days), Volume This Week, Personal Record, 1RM Leaderboard, Top Durations, Top Distances, Water Intake (14 Days). Each tile supports: custom title, icon, size (small/medium/large), accent color and placement (none/left/top/right/bottom), and optional background tint.

System Preferences

• Theme — Light or Dark. (The Stick Figure Editor, Animation Studio, and Match Game always stay light regardless of this setting.) • Image Set — chooses the built-in anatomy images used in workout and muscle views — Option 1, Option 2, or None. • Weight Unit — lbs or kg. Affects all weight display and entry throughout the app — logs, stat cards, personal records. • Distance Unit — mi or km. Affects distance display in stats and complications. • Keep Screen On During Sessions — prevents the screen from locking during active timers, workouts, and voice trainer sessions. Uses more battery.

iCloud Sync

Your data syncs automatically to your iCloud account so it's available on every device signed in with the same Apple ID — and survives a reinstall. • Account — shows your current iCloud status. Sync requires you to be signed in to iCloud on the device. • Last Sync — when the most recent push/pull completed. • Sync Now — pulls the latest workouts, logs, exercises, water, and settings from iCloud. Use this if a reinstall didn't restore your data, or after a long offline period. • Sync Appearance Settings — when on, nav bar and dashboard colors also sync across your devices. Turn off if you want different looks per device. What syncs: workout logs, routines, exercises, muscles, equipment, timers, voice trainer configs, training plans, water logs, journal entries, and (optionally) appearance settings.

Apple Health

• Export Workouts to Health — sends completed workout sessions to the Health app as HKWorkouts (with heart-rate and calories when available). • Use Apple Watch for Workouts — when on, starting a workout on the phone runs a live session on your Watch for accurate heart-rate and calorie tracking. Falls back to an iPhone estimate when the Watch isn't reachable. • Mirror Log — diagnostic log showing the iPhone↔Watch handshake for mirrored workout sessions. • Import Activities — opens a chooser to pick which HealthKit activity types pull into your Log (running, cycling, hiking, swimming, etc.). • Body Weight — optional. Used to tighten calorie estimates for exported workouts. Leave blank to use the default ~70 kg baseline. • Last Synced — relative time since the most recent HealthKit sync. • Sync Now — manually triggers an import (from Health) and an export (to Health) of pending logs.

Water

• Daily Goal — your target intake for the day, in your chosen unit. • Unit — oz or mL. Used everywhere water is shown. • Export to Apple Health — when on, every logged entry is also written to the Health app as a water intake sample. • Import from Apple Health — when on, water samples logged elsewhere (other apps, Siri, Watch) are pulled into Extend. • Last Synced — relative time since the most recent Apple Health water sync. • Sync Now — manually triggers an import from Apple Health (only shown when Import is enabled).

Data

• Export Workouts — exports selected workout routines as a JSON file. Useful as a backup or for transferring to another device. Exports routine definitions only, not logged session history. • Import Workouts — imports workout routines from a previously exported JSON file. Missing exercises, muscles, or equipment are created automatically. • Export Plans — exports selected training plans as a JSON file, including all referenced workouts, exercises, muscles, equipment, voice trainer configs, and timer configs. • Import Plans — imports training plans from a previously exported plan JSON file. All dependencies are resolved by name — created if missing, matched if found. • Export Log Data — exports your full session history as a CSV file. • Erase All Data (including iCloud) — wipes everything: logs, routines, exercises, muscles, equipment, timers, voice trainer configs, training plans, and all customizations. This also clears your iCloud copy and syncs the empty state to your other devices. Workouts in Apple Health will reimport if Import is enabled. Cannot be undone — this is not the same as deleting and reinstalling the app (a reinstall would restore your data from iCloud).

Apple Watch

Extend on your wrist — pages, complications, and watch-side settings.

▶

Overview

The Watch app mirrors what you care about on your wrist: • Swipe through pages for Today's Plan, Library (quick-start), Steps & Distance, Water, and Settings. • Add complications to your watch face for at-a-glance stats and one-tap deep links. • Mirror an iPhone workout session to capture accurate heart rate and calories. Long-press your watch face → Edit → Complications → scroll to find Extend to add complications.

Pages (Watch App)

Swipe horizontally to move between pages. Hide pages you don't use in Settings → Pages on the Watch — Settings always stays visible so you can re-enable them. • Today's Plan — your scheduled items for today, with check-off tracking. • Library — quick-launch any workout, exercise, timer, or voice trainer config from your library. • Steps & Distance — today's totals with progress toward your goals. • Water — current intake with quick-add buttons. • Settings — pages, goals, complication appearance, voice countdown, diagnostics.

Watch Settings — Pages

Toggle which pages appear when swiping. Hidden pages won't show, but Settings is always available so nothing is unrecoverable.

Watch Settings — Workouts

• Voice Countdown — speaks the last 5 seconds of timed sets and announces complex rounds during workouts run on the Watch.

Watch Settings — Goals

• Steps — your daily step goal (used by the Steps complication and page). • Distance — your daily distance goal. • Unit — mi or km. Affects the Distance complication and Steps & Distance page.

Watch Settings — Complication Shape

Choose the shape used by each complication: Ring (default), Circle, Square, Heart, Star, Hexagon, Shield, Seal, Diamond, Triangle, Pentagon, or Octagon. Configurable per complication (Steps, Distance, Steps & Distance, Water, Today's Plan, Library).

Watch Settings — Complication Color

Pick the shape (outline/fill) color per complication. "Watch Face" follows your watch face's tint; pick a specific color (White, Orange, Blue, Green, Pink) to override.

Watch Settings — Complication Text Color

Pick the color of the value shown in the middle of each complication. "Watch Face" follows the shape color.

Watch Settings — Diagnostics

• Mirror Log — recent handshake messages between the iPhone and Watch (useful when debugging a mirrored workout). • Version — the Watch app's version and build. Confirm it matches the iPhone build before chasing a sync issue.

Complication — Today's Plan

Shows a ring filling up as you complete today's scheduled items. Shows number completed vs. total count. Shows "Rest" on rest days and a full checkmark when everything is done. Tapping opens the Today's Plan page.

Complication — Steps

A ring showing your step count progress toward your daily step goal. The ring turns green when you hit your goal. Numbers over 1,000 are shown compactly (e.g. 8.4k). Set your step goal in the Watch's Settings → Goals.

Complication — Distance

A ring showing how far you've walked or run today toward your daily distance goal. Displays in km or miles depending on your Goals setting. The ring turns green when you hit your goal.

Complication — Steps & Distance

Combines both into one complication — the ring tracks steps, with your current distance shown as a second line below.

Complication — Water

Shows today's water intake as a percentage of your daily goal. Turns green when your goal is reached. Set your water goal on the iPhone in Settings → Water.

Complication — Library (Extend)

Shows the app's name and displays the number of activities you've completed for the day. Tapping opens directly to the Library page for kickstarting activities.

Mirrored Workouts

When "Use Apple Watch for Workouts" is on (iPhone Settings → Apple Health), starting a workout on the phone runs a live HKWorkoutSession on your Watch. The Watch overlay shows duration, heart rate, and calories. Stop / Finish from either device saves the session; the system X button on the Watch prompts to discard.

Dashboard

Home screen with customizable stat tiles and shortcuts.

▶

Tiles

The Dashboard is a grid of tiles you build yourself. Choose which ones, and in which order they appear. Each tile can be a module shortcut, a stat card, or a shortcut to a specific workout, exercise, timer, or voice trainer config. Sizes: small (1 column), medium (2 columns), large (full row).

Stat Cards

• Today's Plan — your scheduled activities for today, with quick-launch. • Favorite Exercise — top 5 exercises you perform most often, as a horizontal bar chart. • Favorite Day — bar chart by day of week. • Workout Frequency — 14-day daily activity dots, plus your longest and current workout streaks listed underneath. • Muscle Group Distribution — interactive donut chart, last 7 days. Long-press a slice to highlight it and see its label and percentage. • Volume This Week — bar chart, last 7 weeks. Tap a bar to highlight and see its value. Configurable per Workout or Exercise. • Personal Record — recent PRs. Configurable per Exercise. • 1RM Leaderboard — top estimated one-rep maxes. Configurable per Exercise. • Top Durations / Top Distances — longest sessions by duration or distance. Configurable per Exercise. • Water Intake (14 Days) — daily bar chart of water logged vs. goal. Tap a bar to highlight. A dashed goal line marks your daily target.

Shortcut Tiles

A Shortcut tile links to a specific saved workout, exercise, timer, or voice trainer config. Workout, exercise, timer, and voice trainer shortcuts use a 3-piece tile: • Top — tap to launch the item directly. • Bottom-left 📊 — opens stats for that item. • Bottom-right 🕐 — opens session history for that item.

Tile Deep Dive (tap to expand)

Tapping a stat card opens a full-screen detail view with a time-range picker (7D / 1M / 3M / 6M / 1Y / All) so you can scope the data to any window. Supported stat cards: • Personal Record — full leaderboard of your top PRs (up to 100 entries). • 1RM Leaderboard — full estimated one-rep max leaderboard. • Top Durations — longest-duration sessions, ranked. • Top Distances — longest-distance sessions, ranked. • Favorite Exercise — full ranked list of exercises by frequency, with last-performed date. • Volume This Week — expanded volume graph that re-buckets automatically based on the selected range (daily, weekly, or monthly). • Muscle Group Distribution — donut chart over the selected range, with tappable slices and percentages. The tile's title, icon, and accent color carry through to the detail view.

Creating a Workout

Each group gets a color and a type: • Complex — exercises performed back to back with no rest, usually using the same weight/implement throughout. • Superset — two or more exercises performed back to back, typically targeting opposing muscle groups. • Circuit — three or more exercises cycled through in sequence, usually for conditioning or time efficiency. ⓘ Button (during session)

The app tracks PRs, estimated one-rep maxes, and Volume automatically as you log sets. View them on the Dashboard (stat cards) or per-exercise in the Exercises module.

Gear

Track wearable / consumable gear like shoes and bikes with ownership dates, mileage, and retirement thresholds.

▶

What Gear Is (and Isn't)

Gear is for items with an ownership window and cumulative wear — shoes, bikes, straps, watches. Unlike Equipment, Gear is not attached to individual workout logs at log time. Instead, each gear item resolves usage on the fly by matching a log's date to its ownership window and the log's exercises to its linked activities. Why the split: a new pair of running shoes should automatically count every run from the day you got them, without you tagging every past log — and old runs should keep their prior gear (or none). Doing that with the Equipment model would require rewriting historical logs. Gear resolves at read time, so nothing is mutated.

Header Buttons

• ➕ — add a new gear item.

Row Buttons

Each gear row shows buttons: • ⭐ — favorite / unfavorite. • 🕐 — history of qualifying logs for this gear. • 📊 — stats (lifetime + selected time range). • ✏️ — edit name, brand, icon, activities, dates, threshold.

Favorites Grid

Favorited gear appears as 3-piece tiles at the top: • Top — tap to open stats. • Bottom-left 📊 — stats. • Bottom-right 🕐 — history.

Adding & Editing Gear

Tap ➕. Fill in: • Name (e.g. Nike Pegasus 40). • Brand (optional). • Icon — pick an SF Symbol. • Linked Activities — the exercises this gear applies to (e.g. Running for a pair of shoes). Only logs of these exercises inside the ownership window count toward this gear's stats. • First used — the day you started using this gear. • Retired — toggle on and pick a date when you stop using it, so a replacement pair's stats don't overlap. • Retirement Threshold (optional) — target distance (e.g. 500 mi). The stats screen surfaces a progress bar and warns as lifetime distance approaches the threshold.

Stats

Opens a stats sheet with: • Lifetime — total sessions, cumulative distance, and start date. Always full history, not affected by the time-range picker. • Retirement Threshold — progress bar toward the target distance (only shown if set). Turns orange at 90%, red at 100%. • Time Range Picker — 7D / 1M / 3M / 6M / 1Y / All. Filters the range summary below (sessions, distance, duration). • Linked Activities — the exercises this gear resolves against.

History

Lists every log inside the selected time range whose date falls within the gear's ownership window AND that includes at least one linked activity. Each row shows the date, workout name, and the matching exercises with their distance when available.

Retiring & Replacing

When you get a new pair, edit the old pair, toggle Retired on, and pick the last-used date. Then add the new pair with its own start date — the two won't overlap. Retired gear stays visible in the list (marked "Retired") so its lifetime stats remain accessible.

Gear vs Equipment

• Equipment = static gym items (barbell, rower, dumbbell). Attached to a workout log explicitly at log time. Filters and history use the tagged IDs. • Gear = items you own with a lifespan (shoes, bikes, wearables). Resolved dynamically from ownership dates + linked activities. Never mutates prior logs when you add or replace an item. If you're tempted to give an Equipment item a start date, it's probably Gear.

Water

Track daily water intake with goals, history, and Apple Health sync.

▶

Logging Water

Tap one of the quick-add buttons (+4 oz, +8 oz, etc.) to instantly add water. Or enter a custom amount. Each entry is timestamped and saved to your history.

Daily Goal

The large water droplet shows today's progress toward your daily goal. The percentage and total logged amount update as you add entries. The droplet fills and turns solid blue when you hit your goal. Set your goal in Settings → Water → Daily Goal.

History

Tap History to view all past entries. Filter by week, month, or year. Tap the pencil ✏️ on any row to edit an entry, or the trash 🗑 to delete it. A confirmation appears before deleting.

Bar Chart

The chart shows daily intake for the selected range. Bars that reach your goal appear in solid blue; partial days are lighter. A dashed goal line marks your daily target. Tap any bar to see the exact value and date.

Apple Health Sync

In Settings → Water, enable Export to Apple Health to send logged entries to the Health app, or Import from Apple Health to pull water data in. Tap Sync Now to trigger a manual sync.

Dashboard Stat Card

Add the Water Intake (14 days) stat card to your Dashboard to see your recent hydration at a glance. It shows a bar chart of daily totals with a goal line.

Log Module Integration

Water entries appear as blue pills on calendar day cells, blue dots in the Activity Ribbon, and blue-edged cards in the Timeline view. Tapping any water entry in the Log opens the filtered water history for that day.

Extras

Fun extras built into the app.

▶

Match 3 — mini game

A match-3 puzzle game with 1000 levels. Swap adjacent tiles to match 3 or more of the same type. Larger matches earn powerups. If you get stuck, a tile will visibly shake as a hint. If you can't beat a level first try, no worries — the next attempt(s) will have more moves available. Your level saves automatically. Add the Match 3 tile to your Dashboard to see your current level at a glance.

Stick Figure Animator

Build frame-by-frame stick figure animations, export as GIFs, and attach them to exercises as how-to references. • Frame Editor — drag joints to pose the figure. Use X/Y controls to position it on the canvas. Adjust scale, limb thickness, and body proportions with the sliders below. • Saving Frames — tap "Save Frame", give it a name. Open the frame list to load, copy, rename, or delete frames. • Animation Studio — tap "Open Studio" to arrange frames into a sequence, set speed, preview, and export as a GIF. • Adding Objects — tap "Add Object" to place emojis, images, or colored boxes on the canvas. Each object can be moved, scaled, and rotated independently.

Empty Tiles on Dashboard

Add an empty tile to the dashboard and choose an emoticon to go with it. Wind it up counter-clockwise and let it go to spin forward. For your OCD, it keeps track of how many times you've done it.

Privacy

Privacy as a first principle — short version below, full policy linked.

Extend collects nothing. No accounts. No analytics. No tracking SDKs. No ads. No third-party services.

What lives where:

  • On your device — all workout logs, routines, exercises, plans, water, journal, and preferences.
  • Your iCloud (optional) — if iCloud sync is on, data syncs to your private iCloud database across your own devices. Apple's terms govern that storage. We can't see it.
  • Apple HealthKit (optional) — opt-in import / export of workouts and water. Stays on your device.
  • Apple Watch — data flows between iPhone and Watch over WatchConnectivity. No external server is involved.
  • Exports — JSON / CSV files go wherever you choose to save them.
